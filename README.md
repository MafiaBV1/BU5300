### Oppgave 10 – Change Management Plan (PROSCI / ADKAR)

Documents a structured response to a GDPR-related regulatory change imposed by Datatilsynet, using the **PROSCI three-phase model** and **ADKAR framework**:

**ADKAR (individual level):**
| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| **A**wareness | Team understands *why* the GDPR change is required |
| **D**esire | Team members *want* to support the compliance change |
| **K**nowledge | Team *knows* how to implement the required changes |
| **A**bility | Team *can* execute the technical and procedural changes |
| **R**einforcement | Compliance is *sustained* and monitored over time |

**PROSCI three-phase model (organisational level):**
1. **Preparing for Change** — Identify resistance, assess team capacity, map stakeholders
2. **Managing Change** — Implement, communicate, train; linked to sprint planning
3. **Reinforcing Change** — Collect feedback, celebrate milestones, adjust processes

Includes a stakeholder communication table with responsible parties (Sofie, Thea, Martin D., Merethe, Ole) and a risk cross-reference to R6 from Oppgave 8.

### Individual Reflection Note – Candidate 356

A ~1-page individual reflection covering:
- Role as **marketing lead (WP8)** and contributions across deliverables
- Lessons from the cost analysis: distinguishing one-time vs. recurring costs, estimating without historical data, anchoring forecasts in explicit assumptions
- How stakeholder analysis shapes communication strategy — different audiences (renters vs. Datatilsynet) require fundamentally different messaging and channels
- PROSCI/ADKAR applied in practice: structuring change as a systematic process rather than reacting ad hoc
- Key insight: IT project failures are rarely purely technical — they have a human and communicative dimension requiring equal attention
